Featured Projects

Research that connects models, machines and decisions.

These projects share a practical aim: make AI work where data are sparse, processes drift and expert knowledge still matters.

CSIRO / Additive Manufacturing

Knowledge-Guided AI for Additive Manufacturing

Data-efficient learning pipelines for directed energy deposition, including few-shot adaptation under process-condition drift and prior-guided parameter selection. The work aims to support future closed-loop control and quality-assured production.

Digital Twin / Systems Integration

Service-Oriented Digital Twin for Additive Manufacturing

A practical digital twin framework developed from minimal viable bidirectional information flow. It connects sensing, AI modules, data infrastructure and manufacturing decisions while allowing capability to grow incrementally.

LLMs / Knowledge Engineering

Manufacturing Knowledge Engineering with LLMs

Retrieval-augmented AI tools for machine-specific knowledge access, parameter guidance, troubleshooting and operator training. The goal is to help experts retrieve, connect and validate fragmented manufacturing knowledge.

Computer Vision / Monitoring

AI Vision for 3D Printing Quality Prediction

Vision-based deep learning methods that use early printing observations to predict future warping before failure becomes visible, supporting earlier intervention in additive manufacturing workflows.

Robotics / Agricultural AI

Robotics Vision for Occluded Tree Branches

Occlusion-aware machine vision for apple harvesting robots, using structural references to infer hidden branch geometry in complex outdoor environments and support safer robotic path planning.

Leadership / Student Teams

Mentoring AI and Robotics Student Teams

Advising student teams and industry-linked projects across automation, rover vision, drone search and rescue, and manufacturing monitoring. The focus is helping early-career engineers turn AI ideas into practical systems.

About

Building useful AI for physical systems.

I am a researcher working at the intersection of AI, robotics, manufacturing and digital twins. My background in mechanical engineering shapes how I approach AI: useful intelligence must respect machines, sensors, materials, operators, constraints and incomplete real-world information.

At CSIRO, my current work focuses on data-efficient and deployable AI for additive manufacturing, including process-condition drift adaptation, melt-pool representation learning, real-time decision support and knowledge-guided tools for future quality-assured production.

AI + Manufacturing Small-data learning, process knowledge and deployment-oriented workflows.
Digital Twins Service-oriented and incremental twin frameworks for additive manufacturing.
Robotics Vision Occlusion-aware perception for agricultural robotics and physical environments.
Mentoring Student teams, industry projects and community leadership in AI and engineering.
Mentoring & Community

Making AI capability shared, visible and usable.

I mentor students and industry-facing teams on automation, monitoring, data collection, knowledge capture and AI adoption. Across CSIRO innovation programs, Monash Industry Innovation Projects, factory visits and manufacturing panels, my focus is often to help teams define the right problem: what to measure, what decision the AI should support and how the solution can fit existing machines and people.

I am also committed to being visible in fields where women remain underrepresented: manufacturing, advanced engineering and technical leadership. I want students and industry partners to see that hands-on manufacturing knowledge and AI expertise can come from women, and that the future of manufacturing should be more open than its past.
